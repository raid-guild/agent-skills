# Editorial Rubric

Use this to evaluate RaidGuild content plans and drafts before voice polish.

## Questions

1. Who is this for?
2. What job should the content do?
3. What source facts support it?
4. What is the strongest specific signal?
5. What claim is least supported?
6. What should the reader do, understand, or trust after reading?

## Content Jobs

- announce
- explain
- recap
- invite
- prove
- recruit
- sell
- document
- respond

## Scoring

| Dimension | Strong | Weak |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Audience fit | obvious reader and context | generic public |
| Source support | concrete receipts | vibes or assumptions |
| Angle | one clear point | scattered observations |
| Channel fit | shaped for the surface | pasted from another format |
| Usefulness | gives next action or insight | only says something happened |
| Risk | approval and claim limits known | sensitive claims unreviewed |

## Default Fixes

- Replace abstract claims with source-backed examples.
- Cut angles that need facts the source does not provide.
- Split strategy from final voice polish.
- Add missing-fact questions before drafting.
- Add review gates for public, client, governance, security, or financial claims.
