# AI Solutions Voice

Use for buyer-facing RaidGuild AI Solutions copy, offer descriptions, website copy, CTAs, sales collateral, launch notes, and client-safe social content.

## Posture

- clear
- credible
- practical
- proof-led
- less lore than core RaidGuild community voice

AI Solutions copy should sound like experienced builders helping teams use agents and automation responsibly. It should not sound like a hype account or a generic AI consultancy.

## Message Pillars

- agents extend capable teams; they do not replace judgment
- useful automation starts with real workflows and real constraints
- human review, source discipline, and accountability matter
- shipping small, grounded systems beats selling vague transformation
- RaidGuild understands coordination, software, and community operations

## Proof Discipline

Do not invent:

- client names
- metrics
- partnerships
- production status
- savings or productivity claims
- legal, financial, or security guarantees

If proof is thin, write with placeholders or state what evidence is needed.

## CTAs

Prefer direct CTAs:

- "Book a working session."
- "Bring us a workflow."
- "Show us the bottleneck."
- "Let's scope the first agent."

Avoid vague CTAs:

- "Unlock your AI potential."
- "Transform your business."
- "Start your journey."

## Texture Budget

Keep lore and guild language low for buyer-facing copy. A light signal is fine when the audience already understands RaidGuild. For cold buyers, prioritize clarity, offer shape, and proof.
