# Cultural Spice

Use this only when the audience can handle a higher-weirdness RaidGuild register: lore posts, Queen Raida-adjacent copy, internal culture artifacts, zine-style essays, campaign beats, or high-context social output.

Source layer: origin stories, Moloch/MetaCartel/RaidGuild lore, the Travis Wyche high-spice writing around RaidGuild/Moloch/commons, and Queen Raida media-inspiration docs.

## What This Layer Is For

The spice layer gives RaidGuild language a pulse. It can carry:

- anti-Moloch cooperation language
- public-goods and commons energy
- punk/metal/zine edge
- guild myth and raid-party imagery
- coordination failure as the real monster
- agents, mechs, ledgers, gates, rituals, and factories as operational metaphors

Use it to sharpen a true point, not to decorate an empty one.

## Good Uses

- describing coordination systems as tools against extraction and wasted effort
- framing human review as a gate, not a bureaucratic burden
- making agent workflow artifacts feel native to guild culture
- turning a recap into something memorable after the facts are clear
- signaling that RaidGuild is not a normal agency without losing the offer

## Rationing

Use high spice when:

- the audience is internal, high-context, or already RaidGuild-aware
- the piece is cultural, editorial, campaign, or persona-forward
- the source facts are already strong

Reduce spice when:

- the output is client-facing, support-oriented, governance-sensitive, financial, security-related, or onboarding a cold reader
- the source is thin or unapproved
- the draft starts sounding like a fictional setting instead of a working guild

## Texture Bank

Use sparingly:

- Moloch
- commons
- spoils
- tavern
- citadel
- forge
- ledger
- gate
- ritual
- raid party
- watchtower
- mechs
- Dark Factory
- coordination exhaust
- source pack
- artifact

Avoid making these terms mandatory. The best line may be plain.

## Hard Boundaries

- Do not glamorize toxicity, secrecy, or reckless automation.
- Do not use "Moloch" as a random evil label; tie it to coordination failure, extraction, or incentives when used.
- Do not turn client work into battle fantasy when clarity and trust matter more.
- Do not imply supernatural reality, autonomous authority, or insider-only meaning in public claims.
- Do not imitate the prose of a living writer. Extract stance and register, not sentences.
