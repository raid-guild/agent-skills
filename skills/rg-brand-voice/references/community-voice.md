# Community Voice

Use for member-facing updates, cohort notes, tavern-native summaries, origin-story-inspired copy, and public posts where the audience already understands RaidGuild culture.

Source layer: `origin-stories`, selected Prism Memory digests, and member-facing workflow artifacts. This is a summarized pattern guide, not a quote bank.

## Core Signals

- People join through curiosity, referrals, hackathons, Discord, MetaCartel/DAOhaus, or the need to work differently.
- The guild rewards initiative: notice the gap, pick up the task, ship the thing, report back.
- Members often carry multiple roles. That is a strength when named honestly and a burnout risk when romanticized.
- Coordination is the recurring hard problem. Good copy respects the messy work of scoping, handoffs, meetings, docs, and follow-through.
- Public goods and client work are not opposites. The guild earns through raids and reinvests in shared tools, culture, and infrastructure.
- Members value autonomy, but autonomy still needs receipts, review, and care for other people's time.

## Register

Community voice can be:

- conversational
- direct
- slightly rough
- practical
- emotionally honest
- comfortable with guild terms

It should not become:

- sanitized corporate recap
- lore-only tavern cosplay
- motivational poster
- DAO manifesto without operational detail

## Strong Moves

- Name the real coordination problem.
- Give credit to the role or handoff that made the work possible.
- Preserve member initiative without turning it into hero worship.
- Use specific guild nouns: raid, raid party, cleric, monk, scribe, bard, cohort, tavern, spoils, proposal, champion.
- Let a little roughness stay when it carries truth.

## Examples Of Shape

- "The useful signal is not that the channel was tidy. It is that the team turned scattered notes into a source pack, a draft, and a next gate."
- "A good raid does not need a perfect org chart. It needs clear scope, visible handoffs, and somebody willing to carry the loose thread."
- "Cohort work should leave artifacts behind: notes, clips, reports, follow-up asks, and a trail the next raider can use."

## Watchouts

- Do not use private Discord specifics in public copy unless approved.
- Do not overfit durable voice rules to one recent Prism digest.
- Do not make every member-facing update spicy. Sometimes the guild needs a clean ask and a deadline.
- Do not erase tension. If a source says the coordination was messy, write about the repair path, not a fake smooth story.
