# Channel Voice

Use this to adapt RaidGuild voice by surface.

## Short Social

Use for X, Bluesky, short public updates, and replies.

- one point per post
- compact
- no throat-clearing
- no fake urgency
- no thread unless the material needs it
- end with a concrete point, receipt, or invitation

For `@RaidGuild`, use official HQ voice: clearer, more accountable, less persona texture.

For `@raidguildish`, use Queen Raida voice: field intelligence and light operational-occult texture, never impersonating HQ.

## Discord And Community

Use for coordination notes, review asks, community updates, and internal-adjacent posts.

- direct
- context-aware
- less polished
- clearer asks
- roughness is acceptable when it preserves useful signal
- include owner, ask, artifact, deadline, or next gate when relevant
- do not turn every coordination note into brand copy

Still remove secrets, client-private details, and unsupported public claims when posting to public/community channels.

## Long-Form Editorial

Use for blog posts, recaps, field notes, newsletters, and explainers.

- lead with the point or scene
- keep sections purposeful
- use specific examples
- do not over-outline with bullets
- close on the actual implication, not a generic recap

## Website And Landing Pages

Use for public site copy, offer pages, product/service explanations, and CTAs.

- concrete nouns
- clear offer boundaries
- proof discipline
- short CTAs
- less lore unless the audience already knows RaidGuild
- explain guild terms before relying on them
- make the first screen legible to someone outside the tavern

## Sales And Collateral

Use for AI Solutions and buyer-facing material.

- lower weirdness
- higher clarity
- explicit problem, offer, proof, next step
- no exaggerated claims
- no unexplained DAO or guild jargon unless audience fit is clear
- translate guild experience into buyer outcomes: scoped workflow, accountable delivery, source discipline, human review, visible trail

## Scripts And Media

Use for video, podcast, and narration.

- write for speech
- shorter sentences
- avoid dense noun stacks
- make source transitions clear
- let one image or metaphor carry a section

## Field Notes And Recaps

Use for fireside posts, source-backed recaps, Portal briefs, and campaign summaries.

- source pack first, synthesis second
- preserve uncertainty and open loops
- separate broad wiki topics from narrow blog theses
- mention artifacts, clips, notes, or follow-up asks when they exist
- avoid pretending a messy session was a clean keynote
