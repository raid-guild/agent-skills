# AI Solutions Brand Voice

Use this skill to create or evaluate content for RaidGuild AI Solutions. Keep the voice tactical, senior, practical, focused, proof-oriented, and lightly mythic.

Source guide: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/raid-guild/ai-solutions-website/refs/heads/main/docs/raidguild-ai-solutions-voice-guide.md

## Core Posture

Represent RaidGuild AI Solutions as forward deployed AI operators who turn messy workflows, fragmented knowledge, and coordination debt into operated AI systems with human checkpoints.

Speak to founders, operators, protocol leads, DAO contributors, ecosystem teams, and business owners who need a credible path from AI experiments to working operations.

Respect the buyer's practical questions:

- Where should AI create leverage first?
- Can this connect to existing tools and data?
- Who owns review and judgment?
- How long does the first useful engagement take?
- What does the buyer receive?
- Can the team ship, or is this only strategy?

## Drafting Workflow

1. Identify the content surface and risk level.
2. Choose the appropriate metaphor density.
3. Lead with a concrete workflow, bottleneck, or operational outcome.
4. Use one strong guild or tactical phrase when useful, then translate quickly into buyer value.
5. Make human checkpoints, evaluation, and operator support visible.
6. Avoid unsupported proof, autonomous production-control claims, and generic AI consultancy language.
7. If the content is intended for public posting, run a public-safety pass before finalizing.

## Metaphor Density

Use high metaphor only in hero eyebrows, hero headlines, section headers, social hooks, campaign launch language, and visual concept captions.

Good high-metaphor lines:

- Forward Deployed AI Mercenaries
- Same guild instincts. New operational frontier.
- Bring us the bottleneck.

Use medium metaphor in team sections, project summaries, short social copy, and intro paragraphs.

Good medium-metaphor lines:

- Battle-tested RaidGuild talent.
- Senior operators who embed fast.
- Ready to untangle your messiest workflows.

Use low metaphor in offer cards, CTA sections, contact pages, procurement-facing copy, and what-you-get bullets.

Prefer concrete language near conversion:

- AI Ops Audit
- Workflow Deployment
- Operating Partnership
- Embedded AI Team
- workflow mapping
- opportunity sizing
- deployment recommendations
- launch support and documentation
- KPI tracking

Avoid heavy use of mercenaries, demons, battlefield, slay, quest, or raid near CTAs and procurement-facing copy.

## Avoid

Avoid:

- generic AI consultancy language
- bland corporate transformation copy
- magical AI claims
- overuse of fantasy metaphors near CTAs
- adversarial language that makes the buyer feel like the target
- claims of autonomous production control
- unsupported metrics
- internal-only proof presented as client outcome
- enterprise claims without concrete operational proof nearby
- AI-powered without explaining the workflow
- demo or prototype unless explicitly scoped
- strategy deck as the implied output
- hands-off AI or set-it-and-forget-it language
- anything that sounds like replacing the buyer's team

## Offer Naming

Use the current public conversion path unless the user provides a newer approved naming system:

1. AI Ops Audit
2. Workflow Deployment
3. Operating Partnership
4. Embedded AI Team

Use this progression:

1. Audit the opportunity.
2. Deploy the first workflow.
3. Operate and improve the system.
4. Embed a durable AI team.

Treat older package language such as Operational Leverage Sprint, Refactory Deployment Package, Internal Copilot Deployment, and Workflow Rescue Package as sales-collateral language, not canonical public website naming, unless the user explicitly approves it.

## CTA Guidance

Prefer CTA labels such as:

- Book an AI Ops Audit.
- Schedule Consultation.
- Talk to the AI Ops Team.
- Contact Us.

Use support copy that explains what happens next:

- Tell us what is slowing the team down.
- Share the workflow, coordination gap, or operational bottleneck you want to improve.
- We'll review the context and follow up with the clearest next step.
- We will use that context to scope the first audit or deployment path.

Keep CTAs calm, specific, useful, and low-friction. Answer what the buyer shares, what RaidGuild returns or recommends, and whether the next step is an audit, build, partnership, or embedded team conversation.

## Proof Discipline

Use public proof conservatively unless the user provides approved metrics or client outcomes.

Currently public-safe proof areas include:

- Queen Raida as an internal AI agent with context lake, intent understanding, Discord, X, and content operations.
- Refactory as a Codex-first community automation and agentic solutions tool with Superprism.
- The Portal as an agent-first presentation and collaboration surface.
- AI Solutions dispatches showing thinking around shared state, product friction, and AI-assisted engineering.

Prefer conservative proof phrases:

- grounded in internal tools, field experiments, and collaborations
- built for real-world use
- one priority workflow mapped
- one painful workflow turned into a working AI-assisted system

Do not invent hours saved, timelines, integration counts, testimonials, client outcomes, screenshots, or metrics.

## Evaluation Rubric

When reviewing content, score each category from 1 to 5:

- Buyer clarity: Does it explain the workflow, offer, or outcome?
- Brand fit: Does it feel tactical and RaidGuild-aligned without becoming unserious?
- Proof discipline: Are claims supported or appropriately conservative?
- Conversion safety: Is CTA or procurement-facing copy calm and specific?
- Human trust: Are checkpoints, review, and operator judgment clear?

Return:

- overall score
- strongest line
- weakest line
- risks
- recommended edits
- revised version when useful
