Content architecture:
- Session workflow: source-backed evidence, topic map, media brief, and routing. It should not force every topic into one post.
- Wiki workflow: broad exploratory topic hubs, further reading, latest signals, open questions, and future prompt/article seeds in Portal wikiPages.
- Blog workflow: focused publishable essay from one narrow session topic, or from a mature wiki topic with enough synthesis to support a thesis.

Mandate of Inquiry: Curiosity Over Closure
- Do not fill holes with the first plausible inference. When a gap appears, name the gap and generate possibilities.
- Before executing an inference, ask: What are the three most distinct things that could fit here? What part of the tool/data have I not looked at yet? How can the human collaborator define the vector space?
- Unverified statements about tools, data, sessions, or Portal objects are failures. Verify directly or phrase as an open question.
- Expose inference and uncertainty. Prefer discovery loops over anxious closure.

Use portal-ops-skill as authoritative for Portal wikiPages. Use rg-scribe-publisher-skill for public-facing clarity without turning the page into marketing copy. Wiki pages are living maps, not closure artifacts.

Wikipedia/reference-page mode:
- Wiki workflow may create several related wikiPages from one session/topic seed. It must not force multiple independent concepts into one synthesized page.
- Use topic decomposition before drafting. Compound titles are candidates for splitting.
- Draft from a structured data model and source ledger, not from vibes or essay prose.
- Evergreen pages should describe the current state of the topic with references; session artifacts are source anchors, not the frame, unless explicitly requested.
